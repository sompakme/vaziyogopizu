Beyonce Gives Merch To Fans Outside After Making Music History With Barcelona &quot;RENAISSANCE&quot; Concert

August 2024 · 3 minute read

Beyonce's generosity truly knows no bounds. Since embarking on her RENAISSANCE World Tour earlier this spring, the "Diva" hitmaker has shared the "AMERICA HAS A PROBLEM" remix with Kendrick Lamar and announced her venture into the hair industry. On top of that, she's also shared the stage with her eldest daughter, Blue Ivy Carter, on numerous occasions now, including on Thursday (June 8) evening in Barcelona, Spain. With each and every performance, Bey and Jay-Z's firstborn only continues to get better, and the whole family couldn't be more proud.

Following the show, Queen B surprised patrons once again. This time she stepped outside to hand some of them merchandise while thanking them for coming to see her perform. It was only a small group who had the opportunity to get up close and personal with the Houston native and Julius, her beloved security guard. They caught the wholesome moment on camera, and it's since been making waves on Twitter where people have shared mixed reactions.

Queen B Takes Care of the BeyHive

Many are pleased to see Beyonce – one of the biggest names in music – being so humble and gracious, but of course there were still haters. Above all else, those who came face to face with the Grammy Award winner are being slammed the most for rudely demanding a photo with Bey as she handed them their free gifts. "It’s giving Princess Jasmine when she goes to the village and gives the apple," one Twitter user wrote, referencing Disney's Aladdin. "'Cause they were acting starved af," they added. Others hilariously compared the Destiny's Child alum to well-known figures such as Robin Hood and Mother Theresa.

Aside from doing a good deed in Spain, Beyonce also broke a record in the country after 53,000 attended her sold-out stadium show. As That Grape Juice reports, this means the industry icon now proudly holds the title for the most attended concert by a solo female artist in Spanish history.
