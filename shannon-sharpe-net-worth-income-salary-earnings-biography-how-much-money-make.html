Shannon Sharpe (born June 26, 1968) is a former American football tight end who played for the Denver Broncos and Baltimore Ravens of the National Football League (NFL). Sharpe is also a former analyst for CBS Sports on its NFL telecasts, having previously worked for the Denver Broncos and the Baltimore Ravens, respectively. He was chosen to the Pro Football Hall of Fame and was officially inducted into the organisation on August 6, 201Sharpe played 12 seasons for the Broncos (1990–99, 2002–03) and two seasons for the Ravens (2000–01), winning three Super Bowls and finishing his career as the NFL’s all-time leader in receptions (815), receiving yards (10,060), and receiving touchdowns (62) by a tight end until Tony Gonzalez surpassed all three of these records, including breaking Sharpe’s total yardage record on October 5, 200Sharpe’s total yardage record was broken on October 5, 2008 With more than 10,000 receiving yards under his belt, Sharpe has the distinction of being the only tight end in history to do so.
