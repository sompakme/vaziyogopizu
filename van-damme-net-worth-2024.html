Van Damme’s Early Life and Career

Van Damme was born in Berchem-Sainte-Agathe, Brussels, Belgium. He began martial arts at the age of 10, joining the Centre National De Karate (National Center of Karate) under the guidance of Claude Goetz in Belgium. His early passion for martial arts led to a successful career in the film industry, where he became known for his martial arts action films.

Breakthrough in Hollywood

Van Damme’s breakthrough came in 1988 when he starred in the film “Bloodsport.” The film was a commercial success and established Van Damme as a new star in the action film genre. His subsequent films, including “Kickboxer,” “Universal Soldier,” and “Timecop,” further cemented his status as a leading action star.
