Belinda Effah biography

Belinda Effah is one of thirteen children in the family, and her father was a military officer in the Nigerian Navy. She was born and raised in a coastal state in Southern Nigeria called Cross River State.

Belinda Effah siblings and her dad have been her source of inspiration. Her elder brother is called Patrick, and he is a Navy Captain.

How old is Belinda Effah?

The popular Nigeria actress was born on December 14th, 1989. As of 2020, Belinda Effah age is 31 years.

Educational background

The actress began and finished her primary education at the Hillside International Nursery & Primary School. She then joined Nigerian Navy Secondary School Port Harcourt.

After completing her secondary school education, she was admitted to the University of Calabar (UniCal). She pursued Genetics and Bio-Technology at the university.

Her flourishing acting career

The actress had always had a passion for arts right from her childhood days. As a young girl, she would pen stories for her friends and sisters to read. While she was in primary school, she was good at mimicking people - an innate ability that works well for most actors and actresses.

Her acting career started in 2005, in TV series called Shallow Waters. While acting in the series, she decided to try the reality show Next Movie Star. Her participation in the show made her quite famous in the country, and her fanbase significantly increased.

Her first major movie as an actress was The Room, which is an Efik language movie. The film was aired on DSTV. Afterward, she contested in a reality TV show and qualified to the final stage, but did not win.

Effah did not throw in the towel; instead, she continued going for auditions. In 2011, she got a role and featured in Tales of Eve. It was this particular show that catapulted her to the limelight. Since then, she has made numerous appearances in various movies in the Nigerian movie industry.

Effah has also worked with popular Nigerian lifestyle and music channel Sound City as a TV presenter. She, however, left the station to begin her own TV show titled Lunch Break with Belinda.
