Luke Bryan reveals the bet Peyton Manning lost to host the CMA's

August 2024 · 3 minute read

The two have great hosting chemistry!

    Luke Bryan and Peyton Manning are gearing up to host the 56th Annual Country Music Association Awards, one of the biggest nights in country music, on 9 November.

    MORE: Inside Luke Bryan's heartwarming family unit with five children

    The two have been showing off their hosting chemistry ahead of the exciting awards, and though they appear to be best buds, it seems Peyton's participation wasn't exactly voluntary.

    During an appearance on to give viewers a sneak peek at what they can expect from the big country music night, the hosts revealed the bet the former football player lost, and subsequently made him a co-host.

    WATCH: Luke Bryan shares helpful farming tip with fans

    MORE: Luke Bryan shares heartbreaking tribute to late American Idol runner-up Willie Spence

    ABC's Will Reeve met with the two down in Nashville, Tennessee, where he explained that after several years of friendship, Peyton and the country singer's friendship was facing "the highest stakes test yet," as they co-host.

    When asked how the idea of the two hosting together came about, the football Hall of Famer was quick to confess: "Well, Luke bet me on the Tennessee v. Georgia game, he said, 'If Georgia beats Tennessee you have to co-host,' so, I just found out."

    His confession prompted immediate laughter and cheer from his betting opponent, who proceeded to praise their hosting chemistry.

    "We have some history together, I think it's going to be loose, and that's the cool thing about Peyton, is he has always been a natural lover of country music and I think certainly that's why it's going to work," he said.

It's going to be so much fun tonight to see those two… It's going to be an absolute party tonight."
